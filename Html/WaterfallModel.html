What is Waterfall Model?
The Waterfall Model is a linear & sequential software development process where each phase must be completed before the next begins — just like water flowing downhill, it never flows back up.
📅 Introduced by Winston W. Royce in 1970
🔗 Each phase produces a deliverable used by next phase
🚫 No going back — phases don't overlap or iterate
📋 Best for well-defined, stable requirements

↓

04

Bug finding, unit & integration testing → Test reports
↓
05
🚀 Deployment
Release to production environment → Live product
↓
06
🔧 Maintenance
Bug fixes, updates, enhancements → Patches

Advantages vs Disadvantages
✅ ADVANTAGES
📌Simple & Easy to understand and use
📄Well-documented at every phase
🎯Clear milestones and defined deliverables
🕐Works well when requirements are clear and fixed
👥Easy to manage due to rigid structure
💰Cost estimations are easier upfront
❌ DISADVANTAGES
🔙No going back — changes are expensive
👤No client involvement after requirement phase
⚠️Late delivery of working software
🐛Bugs found very late in testing phase
🌀Bad for complex or unclear requirements
📦No intermediate deliverables for client

When to Use Waterfall?
📐
Fixed Requirements
Requirements are clearly defined and unlikely to change during development
🏛️
Government / Defense
Large-scale projects with strict documentation and compliance needs
🔬
Well-understood Tech
Technology is stable and the team has prior experience with similar projects
⏳
Short Duration
Projects with a short lifespan where changes are unlikely to occur
⚡ NOT IDEAL FOR: Startups, dynamic projects, e-commerce, mobile apps, or any project where requirements may evolve frequently
